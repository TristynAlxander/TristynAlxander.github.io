Types of Diffusion
	Diffusion Coefficient
		Higher = Easier
		D(x,[])		(m^{2}/s)		Diffusion Coefficient, function of position and concentration
		D			(m^{2}/s)		Diffusion Coefficient, assumed to be constant
		D_{self}	Self-Diffusion: Diffusion without "driving-force" 
		D*			Tracer Diffusion: Track radio active tracer (same element).
		D*_{A}		Tracer Impurity Diffusion: Track radio active tracer (different element).
	
	Steady State: C(r)
	Non-Steady State: C(r,t)
		C	Concentration
		t	time
		r	distance
	
Diffusion Equation (Fick’s second law)
	\frac{d c}{d t}  = D \frac{d^{2} c }{d x^{2}}
		c		(atoms m^{-3})	Concentration at x
		t						time
		D						Diffusion Coefficient, assumed to be constant
		x						Distance

Isotropic Solids
	c(0) Non-Steady State
		Thin-film planar sandwich:		c(x) = \frac{c(0)}{2(\pi D t)^{1/2}} e^{(\frac{-x^{2}}{4 D t})}
		Open planar thin film:			c(x) = \frac{c(0)}{(\pi D t)^{1/2}} e^{(\frac{-x^{2}}{4 D t})}
		Small spherical precipitate:	c(x) = \frac{c(0)}{8(\pi D t)^{3/2}} e^{(\frac{-r^{2}}{4 D t})}
	c(0) Steady State
		Open plate:						\frac{c(x)-c(0)}{c(s)-c(0)} = 1 - erf(x(4Dt)^{-1/2})
		Sandwich plate:					\frac{c(x)-c(0)}{c(s)-c(0)} = 1/2 - erf(x(4Dt)^{-1/2})/2
			c(0)				Concentration of diffusant (Pure Material c(0)=0)
			c(s)				Concentration at surface 
	
	Plot
		y-axis: ln(c(x))
		x-axis: x^2
		Slope: (4Dt)^{-1}
.
Steady State Diffusion Flux (Fick’s First Law)
J_{i} = -D_{i} \frac{dc_{i}}{dx}
	J_{i}		Flux at i
	D_{i}		Diffusion Constant at i
	c_{i}		Concentration at i

	Foil: D = \frac{J l}{c_{0} - c_{f}}
		l		Foil thickness
	D= g  a^{2}  \nu p_{J} e^{-E/kT}
		E_{a}				Activation Energy
		g					Geometric Factor 
			1D: g=1/2
			3D: g=1/6
		a					separation of stable positions
		\nu					Frequency of attempted jumps
		e^{-E_{a}/RT}			Probability of a successful jump
		p_{J}				Probability a jump is possible by virtue of geometry (often: empty spaces / possible)
.
Temperature Dependence 
D = D_{0} e^{-E/RT}
	D_{0} = p_{J} g a^{2} \nu

Energy Barrier for Diffusion
\Gamma = p_{J} \nu e^{-E/RT}
	\Gamma				Frequency of successful jumps
	\nu					Frequency of attempted jumps
	e^{-E_{x}/RT}		Probability of a successful x-jump
	p_{J}				Probability a jump is possible by virtue of geometry (often: empty spaces / possible)
	p_{J} = n_{defect} / N = e^{E_{y} c/kT}
	E_{a} = E_{x} + E_{y} c
.
Correlation factor: f = \frac{\langle x^2 \rangle_{tracer}}{\langle x^2 \rangle_{vacancy}}
